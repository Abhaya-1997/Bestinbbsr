🚌 What Is Mo Bus? (And Who Runs It)

Mo Bus is the brand name for the public bus service under CRUT — Capital Region Urban Transport. "Mo" means "my" in Odia — so it's literally My Bus — a branding choice that genuinely stuck with locals. You'll hear it called AMA Bus too, which means the same thing in slightly different Odia.

CRUT is the nodal agency set up to handle urban public transport for the Bhubaneswar–Cuttack Capital Region, which is why some routes extend into Cuttack. The fleet includes Tata Motors air-conditioned buses and Olectra electric buses — the electric ones are noticeably quieter and more comfortable.

The system is cashless-first but accepts cash. The smart card (the Mo Card) gives you a 10% discount and is worth getting if you travel more than 3–4 times a week. The system is not perfect — timing varies, some outer routes are infrequent — but for the central corridors it is reliable enough to build a real commute around.

💳 The Mo Card Smart Card — Complete Setup Guide

This is the part most guides skip entirely. The Mo Card (also called Mo Bus Smart Card or CRUT Smart Card) works like a prepaid wallet — you load money, tap at boarding, and the fare is deducted automatically at a 10% discount versus cash.

Why every regular commuter should get the Mo Card

  1. 10% discount on every ride — automatically deducted at the tap machine. No negotiating, no rounding up.
  2. No change-hunting — exact fare is deducted every time. The conductor doesn't need to find ₹5 change at 8 AM in a packed bus.
  3. Faster boarding — tap and go. During peak hours this matters more than you'd expect.
  4. Works across all Mo Bus routes — single card, all routes including the Cuttack intercity corridor.
  5. Online recharge — top up via the app without going to a counter. Load once, use for weeks.

📱 The Mo Bus App — A Practical Walkthrough

The official app is called "Mo Bus" and is on both Android and iOS. It's genuinely useful — not just for show. The live tracking feature alone changes how you experience public transport in Bhubaneswar.

AndroidPlay Store — "Mo Bus CRUT"
iOSApp Store — "Mo Bus"

What the App Actually Does

The app has four genuinely useful features — live tracking, route planning, smart card recharge, and pass purchase. Live tracking works well on major routes (Routes 1, 3, 11) and less reliably on outer routes where GPS gaps occur. The route planner is accurate for all published routes.

App Setup — Step by Step

  1. Download "Mo Bus" from Play Store or App Store — search "Mo Bus CRUT Odisha"
  2. Register with your mobile number — OTP verification required. Use the number you'll link to your Mo Card.
  3. Add your Mo Card number — printed on the physical card — under "My Card" in the app.
  4. Enable location permissions — essential for live tracking to show your nearest stops and incoming buses.
  5. Set your home stop and work stop under preferences — gives you one-tap access to your daily routes without re-entering them each time.
Known App Limitation
Live tracking can lag by 3–5 minutes on heavy-network days. Don't cut it too close if you're relying on the ETA. Give yourself a buffer of one bus — if the app shows 4 minutes away, be at the stop in 2 minutes. The route planner is reliable; the exact ETA countdown is not.

🎫 Monthly & Weekly Passes

If you take the bus 5+ days a week, a pass saves you very significant money. Passes are unlimited-trip — you board as often as you want within the validity period without paying per ride.

Weekly Pass — All Routes

Monthly Pass — City Routes
₹350–₹450

All Bhubaneswar city routes, unlimited trips for 30 days. The most popular pass among daily office commuters.

Monthly Pass — Intercity
₹550–₹650

Includes Cuttack intercity routes (Route 11 and others). For Cuttack–Bhubaneswar daily commuters this is a game-changer.

Student Monthly Pass
₹200–₹280

With valid college ID. Available for KIIT, XIMB, SOA, Utkal University students. Carry your current ID card — some counters ask to see it at purchase.

📍 Where to buy passes: Mo Bus app (fastest — online), CRUT counter at Baramunda, Railway Station, or Rajmahal Square, and authorised Common Service Centres across the city. The app is the simplest option for most people.

⏰ Best and Worst Times to Travel by Mo Bus

Knowing the rhythm of the network saves you real time and discomfort. The difference between the best and worst windows can mean a comfortable seat versus standing for 45 minutes in a packed bus.

✅ Best Windows to Travel

6:00–7:30 AM
Early morning — seats available, buses less crowded, GPS tracking most accurate. Ideal for early office shifts.
10:00 AM–12:00 PM
Post-rush lull. Comfortable, less standing, good window for errands and non-office travel.
2:00–4:00 PM
Quietest period across almost all routes. Infocity and Patia routes are significantly emptier.
8:30–9:30 PM
Post-dinner winding down — still running on major routes. Decent for evening returns.

⚠️ Worst Windows — Avoid If Possible

7:30–9:30 AM
Peak morning rush. Routes 1, 3, and 11 fill completely. Board at a terminus (Baramunda or Railway Station) to guarantee a seat.
5:00–7:30 PM
Worst window on weekdays. Patia ↔ Master Canteen and the Cuttack intercity route are brutal. Buses sometimes skip stops at full capacity.
Friday After 4:30 PM
Significantly worse than other weekdays — everyone leaving office early. Add 15–20 minutes to any journey.
Festival Periods
Durga Puja, Diwali, Raja Parba — buses get overloaded and off-schedule. Cuttack route especially crowded during Mahanadi ghats events.

A practical walkthrough for first-time users. Scenario: you're at Baramunda Bus Stand and need to reach Infocity Gate.

Real Trip Example · Baramunda → Infocity Gate
How Route 3 Works in Practice
1
Open Mo Bus app → tap "Route Planner" → type "Baramunda" as start and "Infocity Gate" as destination.
2
App shows Route 3 — Baramunda → Railway Station → Patia → Infocity. Tap the route to see live bus position.
3
Check how many minutes away the next bus is. Walk to Baramunda stop — board from the terminus for a guaranteed seat.
4
Tap your Mo Card at the reader near the door as you board, or tell the conductor "Infocity Gate" and pay ₹30 cash.

Sit back. The ride takes 45–55 minutes depending on traffic. Get off at Infocity Gate — the stop is announced.

🔴 Common Problems & How to Handle Them

  • 🚌
    "Bus didn't stop at my stop" This happens during peak hours when the bus is already at standing capacity. Do not step into the road to flag it down — dangerous and ineffective. Wait for the next one and use the app to see its ETA. On Routes 1 and 3, the next bus is usually within 25–35 minutes even at peak hours.
  • 💳
    "My Mo Card isn't reading on the machine" Hold the card flat against the reader for 2–3 full seconds (not a quick tap). If it still fails, pay cash for that trip and visit the nearest CRUT counter to check card status — sometimes the balance needs a sync update after an online recharge.
  • ❓
    "I boarded the wrong bus" Get off at the next stop, open the app's route planner, and re-board the correct bus. No penalty for getting off early — you've already paid for those stops. The app will tell you the correct route number to wait for.
  • 📵
    "Route 3 isn't running today" CRUT occasionally suspends routes for maintenance, government events near Raj Bhawan or the Secretariat, and public holidays. Check the Mo Bus app or CRUT's Twitter handle (@MoBus_CRUT) — they post disruptions there, usually a few hours in advance.
  • 🔒
    Lost Mo Card Call the CRUT helpline immediately: 1800-345-6190 (toll-free). Provide your registered mobile number. The card can be blocked within 24 hours to prevent misuse of your remaining balance. Replacement card costs ₹50 with balance transferred after verification.

🗣️ Useful Odia Phrases on the Bus

You don't need to be fluent. Even attempting one Odia phrase signals to the conductor that you're a regular, not a tourist — conductors are noticeably more helpful to people who make the effort.

Odia (Transliterated)Meaning & When to Use It
"Ei bus Patia jaucha?"Does this bus go to Patia? — Ask this before boarding if you're not certain.
"Kete stop achi?"How many stops away? — Useful when you can't see stop name boards clearly.
"Master Canteen ra ticket deba"One ticket to Master Canteen, please — the standard cash fare request format.
"Mu neba, thiba"I'll get off, please stop — say this to the conductor or driver 1–2 stops before yours.
"Kete bhada lagiba?"How much is the fare? — Ask this if you're unsure of the slab for your destination.

Final Thoughts

Mo Bus works. It's not the Delhi Metro — timing varies, peak overcrowding is real, and some outer routes are infrequent. But for the central corridors — Baramunda to Patia, Railway Station to Infocity, Cuttack to Bhubaneswar — it is reliable enough to build a daily commute around. Get the Mo Card, download the app, learn your route's rhythm. After two weeks, you'll know which stops add delays, where to board for a guaranteed seat, and when to leave home to catch the right bus. The rest is just Bhubaneswar traffic — which everyone deals with equally, in every vehicle.

🔑 The three things that make the biggest difference: (1) Get the Mo Card — the 10% discount and no-change convenience pays for itself in the first week. (2) Download the app — live tracking changes how you experience the bus completely. (3) Board at a terminus stop during peak hours — Baramunda or Railway Station gives you a guaranteed seat where midpoint boarding gives you standing room only.
